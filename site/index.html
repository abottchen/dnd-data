Chapter I

Passage to Chult

From the House of Healing to the jungle's edge — by oath, by storm, by ice, by augury.

8 sessions 29 fallen
Session I An Oath, a Map, and a Sending Stone — 1 Kythorn 15 MAR 2026
No blade was drawn. The company took Azlund's oath under zone of truth, received the map of Chult and fifty gold apiece, and boarded The Horizon at the Waterdeep docks.
Session II The Horizon, and the First Storm — 5 Kythorn 22 MAR 2026
Days aboard the Horizon: Chumble hid in a pickle-barrel and woke with a familiar named Wickles. The storm came on the fourth day; six hands met it, and every rope held.
Session III Blades in the Salt ✝ 4 10 Kythorn 29 MAR 2026
Sahuagin boarded at dusk. Corwin and Dragonbait held the prow; the company took the stern. Four fell to the party — blade, mockery, and bell. A flash of blue light behind a crate was not commented upon.
Session IV The Ring, Revealed — 28 Kythorn 12 APR 2026
An arm-wrestle unmasked no secrets, but words did. Harlen was revealed a Zhentarim stowaway and the cheese-thief. Corwin showed his left hand, told his name, and named the Ring of Winter.
Session V Through Pirate Steel, into Nyanzaru ✝ 6 29 Kythorn 19 APR 2026
Pirates boarded in the night. The deck ran red. When it became clear the company could not win the tally, Artus Cimber teleported them to Port Nyanzaru in a flash of ice and turned alone to the remaining crew.
Session VI Three Riders and a Ring — 30 Kythorn 26 APR 2026
The party rented three dinosaurs from the Tiryki Anchorage pens, crossed paths with Volo Geddarm on the march to Goldenthrone where they heard the history of Chult and bought his book, and ran the city race circuit — Lilac Mist took first place, and Greig finished third to claim Ubtao's Ring.
Session VII Bones on the Steps of Tymora ✝ 19 1 Flamerule 03 MAY 2026
The party gathered intelligence from the innkeeper and Wakanga O'tamu—who gave Urida a journal and a task—then contracted Musharib as guide to Hrakhamar before cutting down a wave of undead attacking the Temple of Tymora in Malar's Throat.
Session VIII The Stranger Spoke Once, Then Slept — 4 Flamerule 10 MAY 2026
The party spent two days in Port Nyanzaru provisioning and gathering intelligence—visiting Grandfather Zitembe twice for visions and a briefing on the merchant princes, taking on Inete and Undril Silvertusk as companions bound for the jungle—before a stranger collapsed against Chumble in the market, whispered of the wise guardian of Orolunga, and woke the following morning with no memory of having spoken.
Chapter II

Down the Soshenstar to Fort Righteous

From the cooled town to the river's last hex — by canoe, by lasso, by boiled water, by ring-light.

1 session 2 fallen
Session IX Down the Soshenstar by Canoe ✝ 2 11 Flamerule 17 MAY 2026
The party departed Port Nyanzaru on the fifth day, traveled the beach under Musharib's guidance, fended off two waves of flying monkeys, navigated a detour down a tributary where they recovered a magic ring from a Tyrannosaurus Zombie using Wickles as a lasso-bearer, and arrived one hex from Fort Righteous on the eleventh day with water supplies replenished.
VThe Horizon

The Road Ahead

What is known, and where they turn.
What Is Known
  • The Ring of Winter — bound to Artus Cimber's hand, last seen opening an eye of frost on a deck of pirates.
  • The Soulmonger — engine of the Death Curse, somewhere in Chult.
  • The Death Curse — the dead will not stay raised, and the lately-raised waste in their flesh.
  • Azlund's Oath — fifty gold paid in hand, one rare gift each on return, if the Curse can be broken.
  • The Map of Chult — half-marked, given by Azlund at the Waterdeep docks.
  • The Sending Stone — Vex carries the pair, and Azlund waits at the other end.
  • Saja N'baza — a name in Chult, sought by Artus for word of his lost wife.
  • The Yuan-ti's Door — a yuan-ti drew blood and fled within; Wickles watches from the eaves.
Where They Turn

One hex from Fort Righteous, the company means to read what felled the Gauntlet's outpost before pressing on toward Hrakhamar.

Dramatis Personae

Those who walked beside, and those who stood athwart.
Those Who Stood With Them
  • Azlund of the House of Healing, whose oath bound the road
  • Tessa who walked the company down to the docks where the Horizon was moored
  • Rindal first mate of the Horizon, whose tour named the crew
  • Captain Brennan veteran of the Nyanzaru run, who gave them a cloak of billowing for six ropes held
  • Pip cook of the Horizon, who minded the cheese and would not be lied to
  • Corwin, called Artus Cimber who hid his name in a glove and a ring, and turned alone to the pirates so the company might live
  • Dragonbait saurial of many scents, who held the prow when the sahuagin came
  • Torgg officer of the ship's crew who dismissed a crewmate for incompetence and yielded the hiring manifest when Anton asked
  • Volo the wandering chronicler met near the Temple of Savras, who sold the company his Guide to Monsters and bade them find a guide before the jungle
  • Lilac Mist tabaxi dinosaur racer of Port Nyanzaru, whose wayward triceratops first brought her to the company's attention, and who finished the track ahead of them all
  • Kessra nearly lost overboard when the storm struck, caught at the rail by Vex with Anton bracing him
  • Wakanga one of Port Nyanzaru's seven merchant princes, who gave Urida a journal pointing to a magical object in the jungle and asked her to retrieve it
  • Musharib a guide in Jobal's employ, unimpressed by the company's means and motives, who will take them to Hrakhamar at his asking price
  • Zindar gold half-dragon harbormaster, who bid the company safe passage and named the pirates a challenge not yet answered
  • Inete who paid a hundred gold to follow her visions of a red-robed wizard into the Aldani Basin
  • Undril Silvertusk who sought the company's escort to Camp Vengeance, her payment promised only on arrival
  • Grandfather Zitembe of the Temple of Savras in Port Nyanzaru, whose guides were consulted for a donation and who came when his acolyte had fallen
  • Ekene-Afa merchant prince of Port Nyanzaru whose commerce is weapons, and who recognized Lilac by face from the racing circuit
  • Jobal merchant prince of Port Nyanzaru, who controls the jungle guides

  • Harlen Zhentarim spy, the wrong sailor, the cheese-thief, knocked cold in the galley as the party was teleported away
